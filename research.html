Investigating Detection Biases in the Black Hole X-ray Binary Population

Sponsored by NSF CIERA REU under the supervision of Vicky Kalogera and Christopher Berry

We investigate the origin of the lower black hole mass gap, by infering the detection probabilities for a synthesized population of Milky way binary systems.

In the figure on the right, we explore the spatial distribution of detectable systems with respect to the sun (shown with a yellow star symbol).

Exloring Dynamics in Exo-planet Resonance Chains

Supervised by Daniel Fabrycky of the University of Chicago

Resonance chains —systems with two or more resonant pairs— present a unique opportunity to study the migratory history and dynamics of multi-planet systems, as well as guide searches for undetected planets.

In the figure on the right, we present a simulation of the resonances in the Kepler-60 system in comparison to the observed values.

Analysis of Supernova Remnant X-ray Emission

Supervised by Vikram Dwarkadas and Kari Frank

Supernova remnants (SNR) are complex, three-dimensional objects; properly accounting for this complexity in the resulting X-ray emission presents quite a challenge. We apply Smooth Particle Inference (SPI) techniques to analyze these remnants and investigate the progenitor systems.

In the figure on the right, we present an animation of the SPI fitting process for SNR RCW 103.
